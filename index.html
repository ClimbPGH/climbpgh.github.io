ClimbPittsburgh!

Mission Statement

Climb Pittsburgh intends to bring together the appropriate stakeholders in the outdoor recreational community and the City of Pittsburgh in order to provide area residents with

  1. The opportunity to learn the fundamentals of rock climbing in order to safely practice this activity within our city limits.
  2. The access to the necessary equipment and facilities to practice this activity at zero cost to the city of Pittsburgh and zero cost to themselves.
Traditionally, rock climbing has been an activity of privilege dependent upon the ability to travel and acquire specialized gear and mentorship. Our goal is to eliminate these hurdles by installing and maintaining climbing routes within our city green-spaces, and providing interested residents and visitors with open access to these climbing areas.

How Maple Bridge Addresses Liability
CLIMB AT YOUR OWN RISK.
This climbing area is intended for experienced climbers, with the ability to lead an advanced route using their own climbing equipment. Roped climbing is required, this is not a free climb. Obtain proper training and guidance before climbing.
YOU ALONE ARE RESPONSIBLE FOR YOUR SAFETY. REMEMBER - YOU CLIMB AT YOUR OWN RISK.
Rock climbing and associated activities are inherently dangerous and could cause injury or death. Permission to climb here is conditioned upon your assumption of all risk of injury to person and property. Climbing risks included, but are not limited to: falling, collisions with both manmade and natural objects, falling rocks, ice and other debris, failure of equipment or anchors, adverse weather, human error, slippery conditions.

Central Park, New York City

Folks have been rock climbing in Central Park for over 40 years.. The city promotes the activity of one of the many cool things to do when visiting one of the nations crown jewels of urban parks and recreation.
Safety has always been self monitored. As with most other skill heavy “risk” activities, i.e., skateboarding, unicycling, bmx, parkour, etc. the human instinct to self preserve is of paramount importance.

East Gallatin Recreation Area Climbing, Bozeman, Montana

Tucker Boulder Park, Davis, West Virginia

Designed for all residents and visitors to have safes and easy access to climbing opportunities of all levels. Situated in highly visible park locations, these structures are meant to be additional recreational amenities for all to enjoy. Great example of progressive planning and substantial capital investment for 21st century parks and recreation.

As we see in Bozeman and Davis, more and more city leaders are investing significant sums of money to build different kinds of climbing structures for recreational purposes in their urban spaces. What makes Pittsburgh so unique is that within many of our spaces, these structures already exist. We have the opportunity to provide a similar experience (many would say, better!) without building new facilities. In some places, we are merely seeking permission to do what folks have been doing discreetly for many decades now, and in other places we are hoping to install the necessary hardware to practice the activity safely.

Problem : Rock climbing is traditionally a sport that is an exclusive activity. It takes money , expertise, and travel.

Solution : Our mission is to jump through these hoops and bring rock climbing to the city of Pittsburgh at zero cost and in a convenient location, with full involvement from a wide variety of stakeholders.

The Plan

Land For Recreational - Liability for Landowners to Recreational Users

The act defines "recreational purpose" as "any activity undertaken or viewed for exercise, sport, educcation, recreation, relaxation or pleasure"

Phase 1: Sanctining of non-roped climbing (bouldering) in appropriate areas.
Phase 2: Sanctioning of roped climbing along with anchor installation in appropriate areas that serve no purpose in their present state.
Phase 3: Sanctioning of roped climbing along with anchor installation on designated structures where we are certain we will not interfere with, nor compromise in any way, any current utility of the existing structure.
Phase 4: Permission to install hardware and artificial climbing holds on existing structures within and around various green spaces of our city.
